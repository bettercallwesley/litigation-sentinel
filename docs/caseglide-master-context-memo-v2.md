## 4) The Problem Narrative (the language that resonates with executives)

Use these themes repeatedly. They are the backbone of corporate GTM, content, and outbound.

### 4.1 "Drift is the expensive failure mode"

Most blowups do not start as blowups. They start quietly:
- Cases sit longer than they should
- Negotiations do not move
- Counsel sounds confident but nothing changes
- Matters age into expensive phases

By the time leadership notices, the outcome is already expensive.

### 4.2 "Outcomes get set in small moments"

Many outcomes are effectively determined before mediation or trial, in moments that rarely show up in reports:
- Time since last real negotiation
- Whether cases hit key inflection points on time
- Which matters get senior attention
- Which firms push forward vs let cases age

### 4.3 "Boards do not want matter summaries"

Boards ask:
- Are we exposed right now?
- Is that exposure getting better or worse?
- If you could intervene in three places, where would you do it?

Most teams cannot answer those because the truth lives in emails, PDFs, and relationships.

### 4.4 "Venue risk is real"

Some courts behave like "magic jurisdictions":
- Forum shopping is tolerated
- Weak cases survive on procedural rulings
- Extraordinary verdicts stand

Executives cannot always "stay away," but they can stop flying blind by building venue-level visibility into cycle time, severity, and settlement deltas, plus which firms perform in those venues.

## 12) Objection Handling (approved patterns)

Use clean, executive answers.

**"We already have an ELM / e-billing system."**
That helps you see spend, but most teams will tell you this process is not yielding the results it once was, and some might tell you its costing them precious resources that could be better spent proactively resolving cases. Additionally, the data from legal spend doesn't help leadership understand the value the attorney is creating by resolving cases, and complete data is needed to ensure that value is properly measured. ELM/Billing also fail to surface the problem cases until its too late. CaseGlide is the intelligence layer that turns matters into a governed portfolio, not just a ledger, and provides opportunities to turn a nuclear verdict into a smoldering settlement - the thermostat instead of the thermometer.

**"We already get status reports from firms."**
Reports are snapshots, inaccessible to everyone except who was copied on it and who can find it, and the most expensive part of litigation. Litigation Intelligence governance requires a portfolio view that highlights drift signals and patterns across matters, not just narrative updates inside PDFs. Litigation Intelligence also offers the opportunity to reduce most of the time and cost of these reports by distilling the best practices for each milestone report into defined Case Updates.

**"We can build dashboards internally."**
Dashboards without consistent structured capture decay fast. CaseGlide is designed (with 10 years of sophisticated client insight) to simplify data capture by meeting every Case Manager and Attorney where they're at (oftentimes in email and memos). By becoming the foundational litigation layer, CaseGlide ensures it is always complete and current, and turns it into decisions that drive Litigation ROI.

**"Our lawyers handle it."**
That works until you need defensible answers at the board or risk committee level. CaseGlide is about visibility and control without turning leadership into file reviewers.
